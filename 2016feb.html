BETWEEN THE COVERS Book Club is Serious

February 2016

Title:

H is for Hawk

Author:

Helen Macdonald

Discussion Date:

Sunday, February 21st @ 1pm

Discussion Location:

The Peasantry

Roll Call:

Patrick, Mike, Goda

Number of Readers:

3

Book Club Minutes

Now we are really hitting our stride! We completed our second meeting at The Peasantry, very fitting for our book this month. The shared PB&J Pancakes were a huge hit but the boozy cafe drinks were hit or miss. Although we had some people out of town and others that were too hungover to make it, we added a new passionate reader to the club, Goda!

H is for Hawk brought up a mix of feelings. Some of us enjoyed the book, others thought it lacked plot. Macdonald’s writing style is unique, blending in abstract visuals and associating her journey with that of T.H. Whites falconry antics in the 1930’s. The major theme of the book that everyone seemed to enjoy was hearing how hawks were trained. Reading about the tricks and learning the names of the hawk equipment was something that sparked interest in all of us. We came to the conclusion that even though the hawks are trained there is no way that they are domesticated. Hawks are definitely wild animals.

We were able to settle on next month's book with small turnout. This month were going to expand our genre's into science fiction. We also had some spare time to run into the comic book store down the street and see if we could find a graphic novel for future books.
